Parking and Transportation Information
With more than 25,000 parking spaces within a mile of the race track, city officials expect sufficient and successful parking and transportation for the upcoming Honda Grand Prix of St. Petersburg. Parking options include:

  • Park Downtown (Parking garage or street/lot parking)
  • FREE Shuttle from Tropican Field
  • Take the Trolley
  • Parking with Disabilities
  • Entry Gates
  • Motorcycle Parking
  • Bicycle Racks
  • Taxi Stands
  • Street Closures


 Park Downtown: City parking garages will charge a $5-all-day event rate on Friday and a $10-all-day event rate on Saturday and Sunday. Private lots, garages and on-street parking will also be available. Observe all posted parking regulations.
  • South Core Parking Garage ( google maps )
    (enter from the north side of First Ave. S. between Second and First Streets) 
  • BayWalk Parking Garage ( google maps )
    (enter from Second St. between First and Second Avenues N.) 


Park and Ride Shuttle from the Trop: There is a FREE shuttle service from Tropicana Field parking to the race course. Parking at Tropicana Field will be $10. The shuttle will pick passengers up outside the main parking gate at 10th St. and Third Ave. S. and drop passengers near the Hilton on Fourth Ave. S. between Second and Third Streets. There will be a two-block walk to the race entry gate. The shuttle will operate all three race days from 7 a.m. to 7 p.m. 



Take the Trolley: The Looper Downtown Trolley  and Central Avenue Shuttle (St. Pete's Trolley system) will offer rides throughout Grand Prix weekend for .50 per person. The Looper provides a circular route between the city's parking facilities, hotels, restaurants and attractions. It operates from 10 a.m. to midnight (Friday and Saturday) and to 7 p.m. on Sunday, after the race. The closest Looper stop to the race course will be near the Hilton on the east side of  Second St. just north of Fourth Ave. S. The Central Avenue Shuttle serves The Pier, BayWalk, and Central Avenue from Second Ave. to the Grand Central District at 32nd St.

Parking for persons with disabilities: All permanent on-street accessible spaces are available on a first-come, first-served basis at no charge - some have time limits but most are available for all-day parking.  An additional section of metered parking spaces on Central Avenue between First St. and Second St. will be temporarily reserved as free parking spaces for those with valid Disabled Person Parking Permit Placards and available on a first-come, first-served basis Friday through Sunday.  City garages, the USF garage and Tropicana Field provide accessible parking at the prevailing rate.  The USF garage is recommended for those with limited mobility wishing to enter the 5th Ave. S. gate and the SouthCore garage is recommended for those wishing to enter the 1st Ave. S. gate.  The Looper is wheelchair accessible and the Tropicana shuttle includes dedicated wheelchair accessible trolleys.

Entry Gates: There are two entry gates to the race course. Both gates offer wheelchair access. There will be intermittent time when wheelchairs can cross the actual racetrack, with estimated crossing times posted on-site.

  • Gate 1 - First Ave. S. and First St. S.
    • Wheel chair access
    • Ticket booth

  • Gate 5 - Fith Ave. S. and Second St. S.
    • Wheel chair access
    • Ticket booth 
    • Will call



Motorcycle Parking: Special motorcycle parking will be available utilizing a "motorcycle corral" on Central Avenue between First and Second Streets on Saturday and Sunday only.  Spaces are available on a first-come, first served basis for $5.  Motorcycle, moped and bicycle parking are also available in permanent designated spaces on First Ave. N. between First and Second Streets and on Second St. S. between Central and First Avenue S.



Ride Your Bike: A large number of temporary bicycle racks will be available outside each entry gate in addition to the more than 75 permanent bicycle rack locations throughout the downtown area.

Street Closures

The following downtown streets will be closed or have limited access to traffic through race weekend:

  • Closed - Beach Dr. from Central Ave. to First Ave. S;
  • Closed - First St. from First Ave. S to Fifth Ave. S;
  • Limited Access on Second Ave. S from First to Second St. S;
  • Limited Access - First St. S from Fifth Ave. S to Eighth Ave. S AND Sixth Ave. S from Second St. to First St. from March 22 through March 26 2011;

Since mid-February, Bayshore Drive. S.E. from First Ave. S to Fifth Ave. S has been closed to traffic for racetrack construction until Friday, April 6.  Albert Whitted Park is closed to pedestrian and bicycle traffic through April 8.  And the Saturday Morning Market will not be held Grand Prix weekend, March 24,  but returns in its regular location at Al Lang parking lot Saturday, March 31.

Race officials urge everyone around the track construction site to use caution and heed posted signage for their own safety. Marked construction areas are closed sites even when active construction is not present and could result in serious injury if warnings are disregarded.

Throughout race weekend, Albert Whitted Airport will remain open with limited access via Sixth Ave. S.  Limited access to USF-St. Petersburg, the Coast Guard Station, the Port of St. Petersburg, and Demen's Landing will also be available; however, the boat ramp will be closed.
